## Legal Requirements and Documentation for Property Purchase

Purchasing property in Abuja requires meticulous attention to legal procedures and documentation to ensure your investment remains secure and legally protected. The Federal Capital Territory's unique status as a planned federal territory means that property transactions here follow specific protocols that differ from those in other Nigerian states. Understanding these requirements is paramount to avoiding the legal complications that have plagued property ownership in various parts of the country.

The cornerstone of legitimate property acquisition in Katampe Extension is verification of the property's status with the Federal Capital Development Authority (FCDA). Before committing any funds, prospective buyers must confirm that the land in question has been allocated through proper channels and carries valid documentation. This verification process involves examining the original land title documents, confirming that the seller has legal authority to transfer ownership, and ensuring that all necessary government approvals have been obtained for the property in question. The FCDA maintains records of land allocations, and accessing these records through proper channels provides crucial assurance regarding a property's legitimacy.

Essential documents for Katampe Extension property transactions include the Certificate of Occupancy (C of O) for allocated lands, the Governor's Consent where applicable, and survey plans that accurately depict the property boundaries. For properties within planned estate developments, documentation should include the estate development approval from the FCDA, completion certificates for infrastructure, and the developer's own legal documentation authorizing sale of the properties. Buyers should also obtain and verify the current tax status of the property, including evidence of payment of ground rent and other statutory charges. Working with a qualified real estate attorney who specializes in FCT property law is strongly recommended to navigate these requirements effectively and identify any potential issues before they become costly problems.

## Due Diligence and Property Inspection Process

Conducting thorough due diligence before completing any property purchase in Katampe Extension is absolutely essential for protecting your investment and avoiding the numerous pitfalls that can affect real estate transactions. The due diligence process encompasses multiple dimensions, from legal verification to physical inspection of the property, each contributing to a comprehensive understanding of what you are acquiring and any potential concerns that should be addressed.

Legal due diligence begins with tracing the chain of title for the property you are considering. This process involves examining all previous transactions to confirm that each transfer was properly executed and documented, and that the current seller has clear and unencumbered title to the property. A qualified property attorney can conduct this review effectively, identifying any defects or concerns that might not be apparent to someone without legal training. The review should confirm that all necessary consents and approvals were obtained for previous transactions and that the property is free from disputes, liens, or other encumbrances that could affect your ownership rights.

Physical inspection of the property is equally important, revealing aspects of the land and any existing structures that may not be captured in documentation. For vacant land, inspection should verify the accuracy of survey boundaries, identify any encroachments from neighboring properties, and assess the general characteristics of the site that might affect future development. For properties with existing structures, a professional building inspection can identify construction quality issues, needed repairs, or potential problems that might affect the property's value or habitability. Comparing the physical characteristics of the property against its documentation helps ensure that what you are purchasing matches what has been represented.
