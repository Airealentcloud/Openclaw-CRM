## The Yield Compression Nobody's Talking About

Here's what changed between 2020 and 2026:

| District | 2020 Yield | 2026 Yield | Price/SQM (2026) |
|----------|------------|------------|------------------|
| Maitama | 5-6% | 2-3% | ₦1.5M–2.5M |
| Asokoro | 4-5% | 2-4% | ₦1.2M–2M |
| Katampe Extension | 4-5% | 6-8% | ₦800K–1.5M |
| Gwarinpa | 5-7% | 6-8% | ₦400K–700K |

**What happened?** Prices rose faster than rents in prime districts. You're no longer buying income—you're betting on appreciation. That's a fundamental shift in risk profile.

The 18% price growth is real. But if you're buying Maitama at 2-3% yield, you're accepting that your returns come from capital gains, not cash flow. That works if you have a 5-10 year horizon and strong stomach for volatility.

## The Katampe Extension Opportunity

While everyone chases Maitama prestige, smart money is moving to Katampe Extension. Here's why:

**The Numbers:**
- Entry price: 60% of Maitama (₦800K–1.5M vs ₦1.5M–2.5M/sqm)
- Rental yield: 3x Maitama (6-8% vs 2-3%)

**The Catalyst:**
ONEX infrastructure development connecting Katampe to Maitama, Jahi, and major business corridors. Completion through 2026 means this isn't speculation—it's infrastructure arbitrage.

**The Risk:**
Resale market is thinner than established districts. You're betting on continued appreciation and rental demand as the area matures. If you need liquidity in 12 months, this isn't your play.

## The Due Diligence Reality Check

I'm seeing more "distress sales" and "urgent deals" than ever. Here's what I tell every client:

**The distress isn't the price. It's the title.**

When someone abroad hears "act fast, this won't last," the real urgency is often that the title won't survive proper verification. I've seen diaspora buyers lose millions on "deals" that were cheap because the C of O was questionable, the survey was forged, or the seller wasn't the actual owner.

**Your Verification Checklist:**
1. C of O or R of O document (physical + AGIS Portal check)
2. Survey plan (verify at AGIS)
3. Deed of Assignment (properly executed)
4. Power of Attorney (if applicable, properly notarized)
5. Physical plot inspection (with surveyor if available)

If any step gets pushback, walk. There are other plots.

## The Currency Hedge Strategy

Real estate in Abuja isn't just an inflation hedge—it's a currency hedge.

**How It Works:**
If Naira weakens 20% and you hold Abuja property, your asset appreciated 20% in USD terms even if the local market stayed flat. The Naira value of your property rises as the currency falls.

**But Only If:**
- The title is clean (bad title = liability, not hedge)
- The location has genuine demand (not just speculation)
- You can hold through volatility (don't panic sell)

This is why I emphasize verification. A bad title destroys the hedge. You're left with a legal battle, not an appreciating asset.

## My 2026 Playbook

Here's what I'm telling clients right now:

**For Cash Flow Investors:**
Target Katampe Extension, Gwarinpa, and Jabi. Accept lower prestige for 6-8% rental yields and steady occupancy. These are rental demand hubs with middle-class tenants who pay on time.

**For Prestige Buyers:**
Maitama and Asokoro remain the addresses that open doors. Accept the 2-3% yield. You're buying status, location, and resale liquidity. The appreciation is consistent but not spectacular.

**For Growth Investors:**
Katampe Extension and Guzape 2 offer 16-20% appreciation potential. Higher risk, higher reward. You need 3-5 year horizon and tolerance for construction noise and growing infrastructure.

**For Diaspora Buyers:**
Use the current Naira strength as a buying window. But don't chase cheap listings—chase verified listings. Pay 10-15% more for a property with perfect title. It's insurance, not expense.
