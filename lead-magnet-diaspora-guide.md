## Chapter 3: The 5 Biggest Risks (And How to Avoid Them)

### Risk #1: Fake Agents & Ghost Properties
**The Scam**: Online listings for properties that don't exist or aren't for sale.
**The Solution**: 
- Always verify the agent's physical office
- Request video calls from the actual property
- Use verified platforms with property authentication

### Risk #2: Document Fraud
**The Scam**: Forged C of O, fake survey plans, impersonated owners.
**The Solution**:
- Verify C of O at Abuja Geographic Information System (AGIS)
- Cross-check survey plans with licensed surveyors
- Use escrow services for payments

### Risk #3: Double Sales
**The Scam**: One property sold to multiple buyers.
**The Solution**:
- Conduct official land search before payment
- Obtain consent from AGIS for transfers
- Work with reputable legal practitioners

### Risk #4: Uncompleted Developer Projects
**The Scam**: Off-plan purchases that never get completed.
**The Solution**:
- Research developer track record
- Visit completed projects by same developer
- Structure payments tied to construction milestones

### Risk #5: Hidden Charges
**The Scam**: Unexpected fees that inflate the total cost.
**The Solution**:
- Get all-in cost breakdown upfront
- Budget for: legal (5%), agency (5%), survey, consent fees
- Request written fee schedule

### Phase 1: Research & Shortlisting (Week 1-2)

1. Perfect title registration

## Chapter 6: Legal Documentation Explained

### Certificate of Occupancy (C of O)
**What it is**: Government-issued title document
**Why it matters**: Highest form of land title in Abuja
**Verification**: Check at AGIS with plot number

### Right of Occupancy (R of O)
**What it is**: Temporary occupancy right (usually 99 years)
**Why it matters**: Common for older properties
**Verification**: Confirm validity period and terms

### Deed of Assignment
**What it is**: Transfer document from seller to buyer
**Why it matters**: Legal proof of ownership transfer
**Requirement**: Must be registered at AGIS

### Survey Plan
**What it is**: Land measurement and boundary document
**Why it matters**: Defines exact property location and size
**Verification**: Cross-check with beacon numbers at site

### Governor's Consent
**What it is**: Approval for property transfer
**When needed**: For C of O properties being sold
**Timeline**: 30-90 days processing

## Chapter 8: Red Flags: Spotting Fraud from Miles Away

### Agent Red Flags
- [ ] No physical office address
- [ ] Refuses video calls
- [ ] Pressures for quick decisions
- [ ] Requests cash payments
- [ ] Can't provide property documentation
- [ ] Price significantly below market

### Property Red Flags
- [ ] Seller can't produce original documents
- [ ] Property occupied but "owner" is selling
- [ ] Multiple agents listing same property
- [ ] No verifiable survey plan
- [ ] Price too good to be true
- [ ] Reluctance to allow inspection

### Document Red Flags
- [ ] C of O doesn't match property location
- [ ] Survey plan beacon numbers don't align
- [ ] Signatures appear forged or inconsistent
- [ ] Dates don't make logical sense
- [ ] Missing official stamps/seals
- [ ] Photocopies only, no originals
