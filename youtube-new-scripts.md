### Main Content (1:30 - 7:30)

#### Section 1: Understanding Abuja Title Types (1:30 - 3:00)
**[VISUAL: Document examples, graphics showing different title types]**

"First, you need to understand the different types of property titles in Abuja. Not all titles are created equal, and knowing the difference could save you from a lifetime of legal nightmares.

**The 5 Main Title Types in Abuja:**

1. **Certificate of Occupancy (C of O)** - The holy grail. Issued by the FCT Administration, it proves the government has allocated land to someone. Valid for 99 years. This is what you want.

2. **Registered Title (R of O)** - Similar to C of O but processed through the Land Registry. Equally valid.

3. **Right of Occupancy (R/O)** - Issued by the Minister of FCT. Pre-2010 properties often have this. Still valid but older documentation.

4. **Deed of Assignment** - A contract between seller and buyer. This is NOT a government title. It must be backed by a valid C of O or R of O.

5. **Gazette Copy** - Official government publication confirming land allocation. Critical for verifying historical ownership.

**Critical Statistic:** Over 60% of land disputes in Abuja involve properties where the seller only had a Deed of Assignment without a valid underlying C of O."

#### Section 2: Step-by-Step Verification Process (3:00 - 5:30)
**[VISUAL: Screen recordings of verification steps, office visits]**

"Now, here's my exact 5-step verification process that I use with every client:

**Step 1: Request Original Documents**
Demand to see the ORIGINAL C of O, R of O, or Gazette. Never accept photocopies for initial verification. Check that names match exactly—spelling errors can invalidate everything.

**Step 2: Verify at FCDA Land Registry**
Go to the Federal Capital Territory Administration, Department of Development Control. Request a status check. This costs around ₦50,000 but is non-negotiable. They'll confirm if the title is genuine and if there are any encumbrances.

**Step 3: Conduct a Search at Land Registry**
File a formal search at the Abuja Geographic Information System (AGIS) or the Land Registry. This reveals:
- Current registered owner
- Transaction history
- Any liens or mortgages
- Boundaries and survey details

**Step 4: Verify the Survey Plan**
Hire a licensed surveyor to conduct an independent survey. The survey plan must match the title documents exactly. Cost: ₦200,000 - ₦500,000 depending on property size.

**Step 5: Confirm No Disputes**
Check with:
- Local traditional rulers (for historical land claims)
- Neighboring property owners
- Court records for any pending litigation

**Timeframe:** Complete verification takes 2-4 weeks. Anyone who pushes you to close faster is a red flag."

#### Section 3: Red Flags to Watch For (5:30 - 7:30)
**[VISUAL: Warning graphics, "Red Flag" icons]**

"Let me share the warning signs that should make you walk away immediately:

**Major Red Flags:**
- Seller refuses original document inspection
- C of O shows different property size than actual land
- Multiple owners with conflicting claims
- Property has been subdivided without proper documentation
- Seller has only "local government allocation" papers
- Price is significantly below market (too good to be true)

**Case Study:** In 2022, a client almost lost ₦180 million on a plot in Lokogoma. The seller had a valid C of O—but our search revealed the land had been pledged as collateral for a ₦50 million bank loan. We discovered this during Step 3 and saved our client from financial disaster.

**The Numbers:**
